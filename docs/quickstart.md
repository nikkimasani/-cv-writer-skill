## Understanding the Workflow

The skill follows a 5-phase process:

### Phase 1: Information Gathering 🗂️

Claude asks about:
- What role you're targeting
- Your experience level
- Whether you have existing CV materials
- Any special circumstances (gaps, transitions, etc.)

**Tip:** Be specific and honest. The more context you provide, the better the output.

### Phase 2: Job Description Analysis 🔍

If you have a job description, Claude will:
- Extract all requirements (must-have vs. nice-to-have)
- Identify critical ATS keywords
- Assess your fit (percentage match)
- Develop positioning strategy

**Example output:**
```
JOB ANALYSIS - Senior Product Manager at FinanceApp

CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS:
✅ 5+ years product management (you have 6)
✅ B2B SaaS experience (you have this)
⚠️ Fintech/payments domain (you have adjacent - will reframe)
✅ Team leadership (you've managed 2 PMs)

TOP 15 ATS KEYWORDS:
Product strategy, roadmap, stakeholder management, data-driven, 
B2B SaaS, fintech, Agile, cross-functional, user research, KPIs...

POSITIONING STRATEGY:
Lead with your product launch that grew revenue 180%. Emphasize 
B2B SaaS expertise. Bridge fintech gap through payment features...

FIT ASSESSMENT: Strong match (85%)
```

### Phase 3: Content Development ✍️

Claude works through each section:
1. Professional Summary (if applicable)
2. Work Experience - bullet by bullet
3. Skills Section
4. Education
5. Additional sections as needed

**What you provide:**
- Your job responsibilities
- Projects you worked on
- Any numbers or metrics you remember
- Technologies or tools you used

**What Claude does:**
- Transforms responsibilities into achievements
- Quantifies impact (even when you don't have exact numbers)
- Integrates ATS keywords naturally
- Uses strong action verbs
- Formats for maximum impact

### Phase 4: Format Selection 📋

Claude recommends the best format for your field:
- **Tech/Engineering** → Technical skills prominent, GitHub links
- **Business/Marketing** → Revenue focus, leadership emphasis
- **Creative** → Portfolio-centric, more visual freedom
- **Academic** → Publications, grants, teaching
- **Executive** → Strategic leadership, board positions
- **Career Changer** → Transferable skills, reframing
- **Freelancer** → Project-based organization

### Phase 5: Review & Polish ✨

Claude runs a quality check:
- ATS compliance verification
- Keyword density check
- Consistency review
- Final proofread
- Suggestions for improvement

## Pro Tips for Best Results

### 1. Prepare Your Materials

**Before starting, gather:**
- [ ] Old CV or LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Job description(s) you're targeting
- [ ] List of past projects and achievements
- [ ] Any metrics or numbers (revenue, users, percentages)
- [ ] List of technologies/tools you've used

### 2. Be Specific with Numbers

Even if you don't have exact numbers:

❌ "Managed projects"
→ ✅ "Managed 5 concurrent projects worth approximately $2M"

❌ "Improved performance"
→ ✅ "Improved page load time by roughly 40%"

❌ "Led a team"
→ ✅ "Led team of 8 engineers and 2 designers"

### 3. Provide Context

Help Claude understand:
- **Company context**: "Series B startup" vs "Fortune 500 company"
- **Role context**: "First PM hire" vs "One of 50 PMs"
- **Impact context**: "This saved the company from bankruptcy" vs "Nice-to-have feature"

### 4. Iterate Freely

- Don't like a bullet? Ask Claude to rewrite it
- Want more emphasis on something? Tell Claude
- Need a different angle? Request alternative framing
- Found better numbers? Update and refine

**Example:**
```
"That second bullet feels too generic. Can you rewrite it to emphasize 
my technical leadership and the specific technologies I used?"
```

### 5. Use Follow-Up Requests

After the initial CV, you can ask for:
- "Can you make the technical skills section more prominent?"
- "Rewrite the summary to emphasize my leadership experience more"
- "Add more keywords from this job description: [paste JD]"
- "Make this bullet more concise"
- "Can you quantify this achievement better?"

## What to Expect

### Time Investment
- **New CV from scratch**: 30-60 minutes
- **Optimizing existing CV**: 15-30 minutes
- **Quick tailoring for job**: 10-15 minutes

### Iterations
Most users go through 2-3 rounds:
1. **First draft**: Full CV creation
2. **Refinement**: Tweaking bullets and emphasis
3. **Polish**: Final details and formatting

### Output Format
Claude will provide:
- Markdown formatted CV content
- Can be copied to Word, Google Docs, or CV builders
- Guidance on converting to PDF
- Formatting recommendations

## Next Steps

Once you have your optimized CV:

1. **Create the document**
   - Copy content to Word/Google Docs
   - Apply professional formatting
   - Save as PDF (unless .docx specifically requested)

2. **Test ATS compatibility**
   - Use free checkers like Jobscan or Resume Worded
   - Verify all text is machine-readable
   - Check keyword density

3. **Get feedback**
   - Share with trusted colleagues
   - Consider professional review if applying to dream roles
   - Make final adjustments

4. **Tailor for each application**
   - Customize summary for each role
   - Adjust keyword emphasis
   - Reorder bullets to emphasize most relevant experience

## Troubleshooting

**"The skill isn't giving me specific enough advice"**
→ Provide more context and specific examples from your experience

**"I don't have metrics for my achievements"**
→ Claude will help you estimate or reframe without numbers

**"The bullets are too long"**
→ Ask: "Make these bullets more concise - one line each"

**"This doesn't sound like me"**
→ Ask: "Can you make the tone more [casual/formal/technical]?"

**"I need help with a cover letter too"**
→ The skill focuses on CVs, but you can ask Claude (without the skill) for cover letter help
